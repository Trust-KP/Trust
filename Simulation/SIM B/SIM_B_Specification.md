# SIM B — Behavioural Doctrine

## Purpose
SIM B introduces behavioural doctrine into the underwriting model.

The objective is to compare how different underwriting strategies
perform under identical risk environments.

## Institutional Layer Introduced
Underwriting doctrine.

## Model Structure

- multiple underwriting agents
- shared stochastic loss environment
- doctrine-based decision rules

## Key Mechanisms

- doctrine-specific risk acceptance thresholds
- performance comparison across doctrines

## Simulation Environment

- three underwriting doctrines
- identical stochastic loss environment

## Outcome Metrics

- doctrine survival rate
- cumulative profit
- volatility of outcomes

## Relationship to Previous Simulation

SIM B extends SIM A by introducing multiple behavioural strategies.

## Notes

Agents differ only in doctrine rules while operating in identical
risk environments.
